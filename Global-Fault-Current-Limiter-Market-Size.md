Market Segmentation
The fault current limiter market is segmented by type, power rating, and end-user. Based on type, the market is divided into superconducting fault current limiters (SFCL) and non-superconducting fault current limiters. Superconducting fault current limiters dominate the market, holding 67% of the share in 2023. SFCLs offer advantages such as compact size, reduced replacement costs, and minimal energy losses under normal operation. When a fault occurs, the SFCL transitions to a highly resistive state, effectively controlling the current without affecting overall network performance. Non-superconducting fault current limiters, though less dominant, are suitable for specific applications and offer cost-effective solutions in low- and medium-voltage networks.
